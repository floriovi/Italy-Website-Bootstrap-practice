Italy!

Italy, a European country with a long Mediterranean coastline, has left a powerful mark on Western culture and cuisine. Its capital, Rome, is home to the Vatican as well as landmark art and ancient ruins.

Positano

Positano is a cliffside village on southern Italy's Amalfi Coast. It's a well-known holiday destination.

Santa Margherita Ligure (Portofino)

A jumble of pastel buildings, set overlooking the azure hues of sky meets sea.

Lago di Como

Located at the foot of the Alps, Lake Como provides one of the most picturesque sceneries in the world.

Tourists throw £905,000 into the Trevi fountain each year.

Italy has the most Unesco Sites in the world. From Mount Etna to the trulli of Alberobello, to Rome's Colosseum, Italy is packed with important sights.

The Sistine chapel welcomes over 20,000 visitors per day Painted by Michelangelo in 1512, the ceiling of the Sistine Chapel is one of the most famous monuments in the world.
